How to Prevent Yourself from Getting Infected

Always wear face mask

The use of face mask in this period cannot be over emphasized. Face masks prevent droplets from entering your mouth and nostrils.

Wash your hands

Clean your hands often. Use soap and water, or an alcohol-based hand rub.

Social Distancing

Maintain a safe distance from anyone who is coughing or sneezing. Stay at home if it's not really important.

Avoid contact with sick people

If a family member or anyone around you is showing corona virus sypmtoms, call NCDC immediately and be careful not to touch them.

Call NCDC!

If you think you are showing symptoms of corona virus, please contact NCDC on their toll-free call centre or social media.

Frequently Asked Questions.

Source of the virus ?

COVID-19 is caused by a coronavirus called SARS-CoV-2. Coronaviruses are a large family of viruses that are common in people and may different species of animals, including camels, cattle, cats, and bats. Rarely, animal coronaviruses can infect people and then spread between people.

How COVID-19 Spreads

The virus that causes COVID-19 is thought to spread mainly from person to person, mainly through respiratory droplets produced when an infected person coughs or sneezes. These droplets can land in the mouths or noses of people who are nearby or possibly be inhaled into the lungs. Spread is more likely when people are in close contact with one another (within about 6 feet).

Will warm weather stop the spread ?

It is not yet known whether weather and temperature affect the spread of COVID-19. Some other viruses, like those that cause the common cold and flu, spread more during cold weather months but that does not mean it is impossible to become sick with these viruses during other months. There is much more to learn about the transmissibility, severity, and other features associated with COVID-19 and investigations are ongoing.

What should I do if there is an outbreak in my community ?

During an outbreak, stay calm and put your preparedness plan to work. Stay home if it's not really important.

What should I do if I have had close contact with someone that has COVID-19 ?

If you have been exposed to someone that has tested positive for COVID-19, you should self-isolate for fourteen days and follow NCDC's guidelines.

Does NCDC recommend the use of facemask to prevent COVID-19 ?

NCDC recommends that people wear a cloth face covering to cover their nose and mouth in the community setting. This is an additional public health measure people should take to reduce the spread of COVID-19 in addition to social distancing, frequent hand cleaning and other everyday preventive actions.
